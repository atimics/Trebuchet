Welcome to Trebuchet

Before you launch your first token, here are some things you should know.

Your funds are your responsibility.
  • Trebuchet creates an ephemeral wallet to handle the launch. Back up its mnemonic immediately — if you lose it, we can't recover it for you.
  • Anyone with access to the mnemonic controls those funds. Don't share it. Don't paste it anywhere other than a wallet you trust.
  • Make sure your computer is secure. Malware on your machine can read what Trebuchet generates.
Things can go wrong during a launch.
  • The launch pays real fees to Raydium (for the liquidity pools), Arweave (for token metadata), and the Solana network. Those fees aren't refundable if something fails partway through.
  • Network congestion can cause transactions to fail. We retry where we can, but failures still happen — and a partial launch may leave funds in the temporary wallet that you'll need to sweep manually.
  • The free Solana public RPC is shared and heavily rate-limited, so transactions stall or fail more often during congestion. We strongly recommend signing up for a dedicated RPC endpoint from Helius, QuickNode, or similar — most have a free tier that's plenty for individual launches. You can configure one in the app settings.
What we don't do.
  • We don't take a cut of your launch. No platform fees, no taxes. Trebuchet is open source — audit the code on GitHub if you want to confirm.
  • If you go into Customize mode and pair against a non-default quote token, we validate that the mint exists and can be used with Raydium CLMM, but we can't tell whether it is the asset you intended. Double-check the address before continuing.
  • We don't undo decisions. Token supply distribution, Fee Key NFT recipients, lock settings — once a launch goes through, those are permanent.
By continuing, you accept that:
  • You understand the risks above.
  • You're responsible for your own funds and decisions.
  • The Trebuchet authors aren't liable for losses, network issues, or anything else that goes wrong during your use of the app.

What are flywheels?

A flywheel is a quote token chosen specifically because it sits in multiple liquidity pools with other interesting tokens. When someone buys your launched token through the flywheel pool, the SOL or other asset they trade in flows into the flywheel token's broader pool network — meaning your launch can pull in arbitrage volume from exotic pairs across the ecosystem, and price action from those paired assets can correlate back to your token.

When the flywheel token gets bought (because someone is buying your token), the supply of its paired assets gets pulled into those LPs. Later, if one of those paired assets appreciates, an arbitrage opportunity may open up that returns the favor — pushing value back toward your token. Exotic pairs are two-way streets, though: if the paired assets decline in value, your token can absorb that pressure too. There is always risk involved.

Reserve flywheel lower risk

Paired with an ETH-backed token, a BTC-backed token, and a stable-backed flywheel token. The goal is to act as a store of value and a hedge against SOL. Both BTC and ETH are battle-tested stores of value; the stable component dampens volatility. Less downside risk than the Meme flywheel, but typically generates less volume.

Meme flywheel default · cross-network

Paired with popular meme tokens that have stood the test of time and retained active communities and attention, plus the Reserve flywheel itself. Has a higher chance of generating meaningful volume — meme markets trade actively — and the Reserve pair means a Meme launch plugs into both networks at once: arbitrage cascades from Reserve-paired tokens reach your launch too. More downside exposure if the paired memes lose value, but the broader coverage is the reason Meme is the default even for non-meme launches.

Which should I pick?

Default is Meme. The cross-network exposure (Reserve is one of its pairs) means a Meme launch covers both networks at once, while a Reserve launch only covers Reserve. For most launches that's the right trade.

Switch to Reserve if you'd rather optimize for lower downside variance over broader exposure — typically a fit for utility tokens, brand tokens, or anything you're positioning for the long term where you'd rather not be tied to meme momentum.
